    Software design should consider many aspects, weigh various advantages and disadvantages, in order to design a solution that tends towards perfection. Before the implementation of the plan, this was also the focus of debate, exploring the feasibility of the plan. However, no matter how good the plan is, there are drawbacks, and it is just a matter of selecting the best among many options. Programming cannot combine all the advantages in one, and there must be a sacrificial aspect. Often, performance is sacrificed to meet accuracy, code is redundant to meet certain requirements, and even business is invaded. Therefore, code has no dual benefits, and gains come with losses.
